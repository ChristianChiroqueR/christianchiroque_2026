The state of open data in Peru

A critical look at the availability and quality of public data for social research.

Peru has made progress in publishing open data, but a lot remains to be done. Platforms like datosabiertos.gob.pe and institutional transparency portals are important steps, but quality and coverage are uneven.

What works

INEI offers high-quality microdata for surveys like ENAHO and ENDES. Formats are consistent and documentation, while improvable, supports reproducibility.

What doesn't

  • Data in PDF when it should be in CSV.
  • Interrupted series with no explanation.
  • Variables redefined across years with no traceability.
  • No APIs — everything is manual download.
Transparency isn't measured by how many files you publish, but by how many questions can be answered with them.

A proposal

Publishing data without a versioned variable dictionary is publishing noise. We need minimum standards: tabular format, stable identifiers, machine-readable metadata, and API endpoints. The good news is that most of these standards already exist — they just need to be adopted.
