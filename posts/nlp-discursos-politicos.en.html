NLP applied to Peruvian political speech

Embeddings, topic classification, and what I found analyzing 12 years of congressional sessions.

Peru's Congress publishes transcripts of every plenary session. Twelve years add up to thousands of hours of speech. The question I asked myself was: what changes and what stays the same in the parliamentary agenda?

Data

About 4,200 plenary sessions between 2013 and 2025. After cleaning and segmenting by intervention, I ended up with around 380,000 speech turns labeled by congressperson, party, and date.

Model

I tried three approaches: TF-IDF + LDA as baseline, BETO embeddings (Spanish BERT), and fine-tuning over a hand-labeled corpus.

from transformers import AutoTokenizer, AutoModel
tokenizer = AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained("dccuchile/bert-base-spanish-wwm-uncased")
model = AutoModel.from_pretrained("dccuchile/bert-base-spanish-wwm-uncased")

Findings

  • The economic agenda is relatively stable; what changes is the language, not the topics.
  • Spikes in security discourse correlate with media coverage, not with actual crime statistics.
  • There's a cluster of purely performative interventions that adds no substantive content and that no topic model identifies as its own category — but embeddings do.

I'll publish the full methodology in a paper soon.
